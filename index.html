Hi, I’m Will.

I work on computational linguistics and French-based Creoles, with a focus on Guadeloupean Creole (GC), speech technology, and syntax.

Thesis advisor: Jaklin Kornfilt

I My thesis develops an ASR pipeline for Guadeloupean Creole to support documentation and quantitative analysis of phenomena like focus/fronting and complementation. I’m broadly interested in representation learning for speech/text in low-resource settings and in building tools that make bilingual education and documentation more practical.

Recent work spans G2P modeling, PER/WER evaluation, and experiments with BERT/LLMs for classification and structure discovery. I also publish on morphology and data quality in shared tasks.

Research

  • Field Research — Empirical data collection in Guadeloupe in 2024 concerning focus and subordination in Guadeloupean Creole.
  • Guadeloupean Creole Syntax — Complementation, reduplicative focus fronting, and complementation structures.
  • ASR — Transcribing data from my field research.
  • Low-resource NLP — Classification and documentation workflows using BERT/LLMs; data curation and evaluation focusing on scarce data.
