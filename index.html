William Kezerian.

MA candidate in Linguistic Studies at Syracuse University. I work on computational linguistics and French-based Creoles, with a focus on Guadeloupean Creole (GC), speech technology, and syntax.

Thesis advisor: Jaklin Kornfilt · NSF-IRES Research Fellow · Fieldwork in Guadeloupe

My thesis develops an ASR pipeline for Guadeloupean Creole to support documentation and quantitative analysis of phenomena like focus/fronting and complementation. I’m broadly interested in representation learning for speech/text in low-resource settings and in building tools that make bilingual education and documentation more practical.

Recent work spans G2P modeling, PER/WER evaluation, and experiments with BERT/LLMs for classification and structure discovery. I also publish on morphology and data quality in shared tasks.

Research

  • ASR & G2P for Guadeloupean Creole — Field-collected speech → transcripts; evaluation with PER/WER.
  • Focus & Fronting in GC — Complementation, left-dislocation, and information structure from elicitation + corpus data.
  • Low-resource NLP — Classification and documentation workflows using BERT/LLMs; data curation and evaluation.
